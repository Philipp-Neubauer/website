About us

Part of the extended Dragonfly team (L-R: Thomasin Abraham, Sarah Wilcox, Edward Abraham, Yvan Richard, Finlay Thompson, and Douglas Bagnall)

We are a specialist data analysis company, valuing excellent, innovative work, carried out in an inclusive way.

Each of us has a PhD in a scientific discipline and significant experience in our own field. Together we have a broad range of skills to draw on and apply to diverse problems. We like the challenge of complex issues. Our research backgrounds, and our expertise in software development gives us the flexibility to tailor our service to the particular needs of each client.

Dragonfly Science was established in 2006 and now has eight staff. We work in the heart of Wellington, New Zealand's capital.

We are committed to the scientific ethics of independence and integrity. We support principles of openness in sharing results, engaging with peer review and collaboration. Our aim is to provide the information necessary for robust public debate and effective management, leading to better outcomes for society and the environment.

Current work

Much of our current work is in statistical analysis, including projects estimating and reducing the bycatch of protected species in fisheries, risk assessments and ecological modelling. We also have ongoing research projects in software development and the analysis of data generated by high speed/volume data processing. We would be interested in hearing from potential collaborators in this area.

Outputs

Outputs and results can be supplied in a range of forms, depending on the project, and are tailored to the client. We commonly prepare written reports and give presentations, but also deliver databases and design and build software tools for specific purposes. Web-based tools are a speciality.

Tools

We specialise in the use of open source software, with collective experience in R, JAGS, Django, Python, PostgreSQL and Latex. We have developed methods to process the large and complex data sets we use, and improve their robustness and performance.
